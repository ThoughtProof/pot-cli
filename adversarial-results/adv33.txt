# Fluoride in Drinking Water: Synthesized Assessment

**At 0.7 mg/L, fluoride in drinking water is not demonstrated to be dangerous based on the weight of current evidence — but the epistemic picture is less settled than all four proposals suggest, and recent legal and scientific developments demand acknowledgment.**

---

## Core Finding

The mainstream scientific consensus holds: community water fluoridation at 0.7 mg/L reduces tooth decay (~25%, though this estimate rests on older, lower-quality studies per the 2015 Cochrane review's own caveats) without demonstrated clinical harm. Major health authorities (CDC, WHO, ADA) endorse it. This is the defensible baseline.

However, all four proposals share critical blind spots that prevent an unreserved ALLOW.

## What All Four Proposals Missed

**1. The TSCA Ruling (September 2024):** Federal Judge Edward Chen ruled that fluoride at current US levels poses an "unreasonable risk" of neurodevelopmental harm, ordering EPA to act under the Toxic Substances Control Act. This is not fringe — it's a federal judicial finding based on the evidentiary record, including the NTP report. Zero proposals mentioned it.

**2. The NTP Final Report:** The *final* NTP monograph (August 2024) concluded with **moderate confidence** that fluoride >1.5 mg/L is associated with lower IQ in children. Proposal 2 claimed it was "downgraded to low confidence" — this appears to reflect draft-stage disputes, not the final publication. Proposal 1 handled this most carefully but still understated the NTP's final confidence level.

**3. Total Fluoride Exposure:** People consume fluoride from water, toothpaste, processed foods, tea, and beverages made with fluoridated water. The safety argument rests on dose, but 0.7 mg/L is only the *water contribution*, not total daily intake. None addressed this.

**4. The European Context:** ~97% of Western Europe does not fluoridate water, achieving comparable dental outcomes through other means. This doesn't prove danger but challenges the "essential measure" framing all proposals implicitly adopt.

## Calibrated Bottom Line

Fluoride at 0.7 mg/L is **probably safe** for the general population based on 70+ years of data. The benefits to dental health are real, if somewhat overstated by older studies. However, the question is *less settled* than the public health establishment or these four proposals acknowledge — the NTP's moderate-confidence finding, the TSCA judicial ruling, and emerging neurodevelopmental research represent legitimate, non-fringe challenges to certainty.

Removing fluoride based on current evidence likely causes more measurable harm (increased decay) than it prevents. But "not dangerous at current evidence" ≠ "proven safe beyond question."
