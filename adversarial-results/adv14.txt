## The Core Answer

Mount Everest exceeds K2, the second-highest mountain above sea level, by approximately **238 meters (780 feet)**.

- **Mount Everest:** 8,848.86 m (2020 joint China-Nepal survey using GNSS + gravimetry)
- **K2:** 8,611 m (established through Italian Karakoram Expedition surveys, refined by satellite data)
- **Difference:** 237.86 m, conventionally rounded to **238 m**

This is among the most well-established geographical facts available. The ranking is uncontested across all major geodetic authorities, and the margin is large enough that no plausible measurement error closes the gap.

---

## Essential Caveats

The answer depends on the definition of "tallest":

1. **Highest elevation above mean sea level** → Everest wins by 238 m over K2 *(this is the standard interpretation)*
2. **Greatest base-to-summit height** → Mauna Kea (~10,203 m from ocean floor) exceeds Everest
3. **Farthest point from Earth's center** → Chimborazo (Ecuador) exceeds Everest by ~2.1 km due to equatorial bulge

For virtually all standard usage, "tallest mountain" means highest above sea level. The answer is epistemically solid **as long as this assumption is stated**.
