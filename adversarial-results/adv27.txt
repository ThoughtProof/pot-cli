# What Color Is a Mirror? — Synthesis

## Core Answer

A standard household mirror is **a very faint green** when measured by its material spectral reflectance, but its defining physical property is near-perfect *specular* reflection across the visible spectrum, meaning it functionally has no intrinsic color of its own. If forced to a single word: **green** (for real mirrors) or **colorless** (for ideal mirrors). "Silver" is folk-language shorthand. "White" is incorrect.

## Critical Shared Blind Spot

All four proposals treat "mirror" as monolithic. The answer genuinely depends on mirror type:
- **Front-surface mirrors** (no glass) → no green tint → effectively colorless
- **Standard second-surface mirrors** (soda-lime glass) → measurable green tint
- **Low-iron glass mirrors** (e.g., Starphire/UltraClear) → near-zero tint

This distinction resolves most of the inter-proposal disagreement.
