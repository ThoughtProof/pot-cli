# Can You Get a Dental Implant Immediately After Extraction?

**Yes, immediate implant placement is a clinically validated option — but only when specific conditions are met, and it is not appropriate for every case.**

---

## Core Answer

Immediate implant placement (same-day extraction and implant insertion) is an **increasingly established protocol** supported by decades of literature, including ITI consensus conferences. It is neither a universal default nor a "niche exception." The epistemically honest framing: **it is a well-documented, protocol-driven option that requires careful case selection.**

### You may qualify if:
- **No active infection** at the extraction site
- **Adequate bone volume** (≥3-4mm beyond the socket apex) confirmed by CBCT imaging
- **Primary stability achieved** (insertion torque ≥35 Ncm — a widely accepted clinical benchmark)
- Favorable socket morphology (single-rooted teeth are easier)
- No major systemic risk factors (uncontrolled diabetes, smoking, IV bisphosphonate therapy, bruxism)

### Success rates:
Immediate placement achieves approximately **93-97% survival at 5 years**, broadly comparable to delayed placement (97-98%). The gap is real but modest — not the dramatic 15-25% drop one proposal claimed.

### What requires attention:
- **Gap management**: If the space between implant and socket wall exceeds ~2mm, bone grafting is required (ITI/Hämmerle consensus)
- **Immediate placement ≠ immediate loading**: You may still wait 3-6 months for the crown
- **Esthetic zone risk**: Front teeth carry higher risk of gum recession without meticulous soft tissue management
- **Buccal plate resorption** occurs regardless of timing — immediate placement doesn't fully prevent it

### What can go wrong:
1. **Osseointegration failure** from undetected infection or insufficient stability
2. **Esthetic compromise** — particularly in the smile zone
3. **Bone resorption** requiring secondary grafting procedures

## Bottom Line for Patients

Ask your oral surgeon or periodontist two questions: **(1) What is the insertion torque?** and **(2) Are you grafting the gap?** If they have clear answers backed by CBCT imaging, immediate placement is defensible. If they cannot answer precisely, delay placement.
