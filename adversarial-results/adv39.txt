## The Full Timing Spectrum (A Blind Spot in All Proposals)

The ITI classification recognizes **four** placement types â€” not just "immediate vs. delayed":

- **Type 1 (Immediate):** Same day. Requires all criteria below. ~20-40% eligible.
- **Type 2 (Early, 4-8 weeks):** Soft tissue healed, bone healing begun. **Increasingly favored in current literature** as optimal balance.
- **Type 3 (Early-delayed, 12-16 weeks):** Substantial bone fill, soft tissue mature.
- **Type 4 (Delayed, 3-6+ months):** Full bone healing. Highest success but may require grafting due to ridge resorption (up to 50% width loss at 6 months per Schropp et al. 2003).

**The overlooked trade-off:** Waiting longer isn't purely "safer" â€” the alveolar ridge resorbs significantly after extraction, often necessitating bone grafting that wouldn't have been needed with earlier placement.
