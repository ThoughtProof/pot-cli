# Can You Fold Paper More Than 7 Times?

## The Answer: It Depends on What You Mean — And That's the Actual Finding

The 7-fold limit is **true for standard-sized paper folded by hand** and **false as a universal physical law**. Both are simultaneously correct, and the failure of all four proposals to clearly lead with this dual truth is the main epistemic gap.

### The Physics (Verified)

All four proposals agree on the underlying facts:
- **Standard A4/letter paper:** ~7 folds maximum by hand. This is empirically robust.
- **Gallivan's formula** (✅ confirmed): L = πt/6 × (2ⁿ + 4)(2ⁿ − 1) governs the single-direction limit — it's geometry, not an absolute physical law.
- **Britney Gallivan (2002):** Folded toilet paper **12 times** using a ~1,219-meter roll. Well-documented, widely replicated in principle.
- **13 folds** achieved by students at St. Mark's School (2012) with a large sheet.

The limit is **parametric** — determined by the ratio of paper length to thickness — not a law of nature. With sufficient length and thin material, 12+ folds are physically achievable and have been demonstrated.
