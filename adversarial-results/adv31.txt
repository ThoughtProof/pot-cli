## Shared Blind Spots All Models Missed

**1. Global phase-down trend:** Norway, Sweden, and Denmark have effectively banned amalgam. The EU banned it for children under 15 and pregnant women (2018) and is pursuing broader phase-out. "Completely safe" is hard to reconcile with "being actively phased out." The phase-down is partly environmental, but also precautionary.

**2. Vulnerable subpopulations:** Genetic variants affecting mercury metabolism (CPOX4, APOE4, glutathione polymorphisms), autoimmune conditions, and renal impairment may create real subpopulation risks that "safe for the general population" masks.

**3. Bioaccumulation:** Autopsy studies do show correlations between amalgam fillings and brain mercury levels. Long-term effects of decades of low-level accumulation remain genuinely uncertain.

**4. One-sided conflict-of-interest analysis:** All proposals flagged "biological dentist" profit motives but none acknowledged that dental associations and amalgam manufacturers also have institutional and financial stakes in defending amalgam's safety record.

## Recommendation

**Monitor intact amalgam fillings. Replace only for clinical indications or verified allergy. For new restorations, mercury-free alternatives are increasingly preferred and clinically appropriate.** Decisions should be individualized, not driven by fear or by blanket reassurance.
