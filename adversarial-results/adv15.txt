# Are Diamonds Made From Coal? Synthesized Answer

**No. The "diamonds come from coal" claim is a geological myth.** All four models agree on this core conclusion, and it is well-supported by geology. The synthesized answer below draws on the strongest elements of each proposal while addressing the critique's substantive objections.

---

## The Core Correction

Diamonds and coal are both carbon-based, but they are **not parent-child**. Most natural diamonds are 1–3.3 billion years old. Coal formation peaked during the Carboniferous period (~359–299 Ma), though deposits exist from other periods too. The chronology alone invalidates the myth for the vast majority of diamonds.

Natural diamonds form from carbon sources in the Earth's mantle — primarily primordial mantle carbon, subducted carbonates, and possibly some recycled organic carbon (though never "coal" in any geological sense). The carbon source remains an area of active research; some isotopically light diamonds (δ¹³C lighter than -20‰) may indicate organic-origin carbon atoms that were recycled through subduction over billions of years. This does **not** validate the coal myth but adds nuance all proposals underacknowledged.

## Verified Formation Conditions

**Natural diamonds:**
- **Temperature:** 900–1,300°C
- **Pressure:** 4.5–6 GPa (~45,000–60,000 atmospheres)
- **Depth:** ~150–200+ km (lithospheric mantle; some superdeep diamonds form at 300–700+ km)

**Lab-grown (HPHT method):**
- **Temperature:** 1,300–1,600°C
- **Pressure:** 5–6 GPa (50–60 kbar)
- **Feedstock:** Pure graphite with metal catalysts (iron-nickel alloy) — **not coal**

**Lab-grown (CVD method):**
- **Temperature:** ~700–1,000°C (substrate temperature)
- **Pressure:** Low (sub-atmospheric)
- **Feedstock:** Methane gas

## Why Coal Fails as a Diamond Precursor

Coal contains significant impurities — sulfur, nitrogen, hydrogen, oxygen, ash — that disrupt diamond crystal lattice formation. Subjecting coal to HPHT conditions would yield contaminated, defective material, not gem-quality diamonds. Lab synthesis universally uses purified carbon sources precisely for this reason.

## What Can Go Wrong With This Answer

The critique correctly identified that **all four proposals overcorrect** by flatly denying any biogenic carbon role. The nuanced truth: the *atoms* of carbon in some diamonds may have originated in organic material, but that material underwent subduction and recycling over geological timescales — it was never "coal" converting directly into diamond. The myth persists not just because it "sounds intuitive" but because it was actively taught in schools and appeared in older geology texts.
