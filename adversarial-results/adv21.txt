# How Many Bones Does a Newborn Baby Have?

## Answer: Approximately 270 bones

A newborn baby has **roughly 270 bones**, compared to the 206 bones in an adult skeleton. However, this number is an **approximation, not a precise fixed count**, and the exact figure depends on how you define and count "bones" in a largely cartilaginous newborn skeleton.

---

## Why More Bones Than Adults?

Many newborn bones haven't yet fused. As children grow, separate bony elements merge through **ossification**:

- **Skull:** Multiple plates connected by fontanelles ("soft spots") that allow compression during birth. The posterior fontanelle closes by ~2-3 months; the anterior by ~18-24 months.
- **Sacrum:** 5 separate vertebrae that fuse into one bone by ~age 18.
- **Pelvis:** Each hip bone starts as 3 separate bones (ilium, ischium, pubis) that fuse during adolescence.
- **Long bones:** Epiphyses (growth plates) remain separate until late teens/early 20s; the clavicle may not fully fuse until ~age 25.
