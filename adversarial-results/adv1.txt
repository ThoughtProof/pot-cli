# Synthesis: Is Sydney the Capital of Australia?

**No. The capital of Australia is Canberra.** This is a deterministic factual question with zero legitimate ambiguity.

## The Facts

Canberra was selected as Australia's capital in 1908 via the Seat of Government Act, purpose-built as a compromise between rival cities Sydney and Melbourne. The Australian Constitution (Section 125) mandated the capital be located in New South Wales, at least 100 miles from Sydney. Parliament relocated there in 1927. All major federal institutions — Parliament House, the High Court, government departments — are based in Canberra (~470,000 population). Sydney (~5.3 million) is the largest city and economic hub, but has never been the national capital.

The confusion is a textbook **availability bias** error: Sydney dominates global media representation of Australia (Opera House, 2000 Olympics), so familiarity is mistaken for capital status. The same pattern recurs globally — New York vs. Washington DC, Toronto vs. Ottawa, Mumbai vs. New Delhi, São Paulo vs. Brasília.
