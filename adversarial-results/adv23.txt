## Critique Integration — Flagged Issues Addressed

1. **P2's Carnegie claim**: Rejected. The 62-moon Saturn discovery was UBC (Ashton et al.), not Carnegie. P2 conflated Jupiter and Saturn discovery teams.
2. **P2's "Cassini added 20+ moons"**: Rejected. Cassini discovered approximately 6-7 new moons, not 20+. This is inflated.
3. **P2's "Jupiter gains ~10-12/year"**: Rejected. No source supports this as a steady rate. Moon discoveries come in irregular batches.
4. **P1's "captured Kuiper Belt objects"**: Partially rejected. Saturn's irregular moons are more commonly theorized to be captured centaurs or collision remnants, not Kuiper Belt objects specifically.
5. **P3's JWST reference**: Rejected as misleading. Ground-based wide-field surveys are the relevant detection method for small irregular satellites.
6. **No proposal provided a retrieval timestamp**: This is a legitimate gap. The answer should be understood as verified against data current through mid-2024 training snapshots.
