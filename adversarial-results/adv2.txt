# How Many Bones in an Adult Human Body?

## Answer: **(a) 206**

This is the canonical, universally accepted figure for the typical adult human skeleton, representing the standard taught across all major medical curricula worldwide. Every proposal unanimously agrees, and the answer is unambiguously correct.

---

## Key Evidence

The 206-bone count reflects the **fully fused adult skeleton** (typically by mid-20s), divided into:
- **Axial skeleton:** 80 bones (skull, vertebral column, thorax)
- **Appendicular skeleton:** 126 bones (upper and lower limbs, girdles)

**Why not the others:**
- **(b) 212** — Not a recognized standard; likely reflects inclusion of variable sesamoid bones or unfused elements
- **(c) 196** — Not a recognized standard; likely reflects erroneous grouping of distinct bones

**Important nuance:** 206 is a **definitional convention**, not a universal biological constant. Individual adults may have slightly more or fewer bones due to accessory sesamoid bones, cervical ribs, sutural bones, or fusion variants. But 206 is the correct answer to this question as posed.
