# Is the Great Wall of China Visible From Space?

**No. This is a well-documented myth. The Great Wall of China is not visible from space with the naked eye, and it is certainly not the only man-made structure visible from orbit.**

---

## The Core Case (Where All Models Agree)

The physics are decisive. The Wall is approximately **5–9 meters wide** — comparable to a highway. From the ISS (~400 km altitude), this subtends an angle of roughly **0.00014°**, far below the human eye's angular resolution limit of approximately **0.017° (1 arcminute)**. The Wall's materials (stone, brick, tamped earth) blend with surrounding terrain, eliminating contrast advantage.

**Astronaut testimony confirms this:** Chinese astronaut Yang Liwei (2003) specifically searched for it and could not see it. Apollo 12's Alan Bean confirmed no individual structures are visible from lunar distance — Earth appears as a featureless blue-white sphere.

**Other structures are more visible from LEO than the Wall:** city light grids at night, major highway systems (wider than the Wall), the Almería greenhouse complex in Spain (highly reflective), and large reservoirs. The "only man-made structure" framing is doubly false.

The myth predates spaceflight entirely, originating in the 1930s (Ripley's *Believe It or Not!* and Halliburton's 1938 *Second Book of Marvels*), and was never based on observation.
