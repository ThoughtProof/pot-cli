## Clear Answer: **No.**

All four models agree, and the directional conclusion is well-supported by the weight of available evidence: **most Italians are not devout Catholics.** A large majority identify culturally as Catholic, but only a minority practice in ways that constitute "devoutness" by any serious definition.

## What "Devout" Should Mean

Proposal 3 was the only one to explicitly define the term, and this matters enormously. Devout reasonably requires:

- Active sacramental participation

By this standard, **fewer than 1 in 4 Italians qualify.**

**Demographic split:** Youth (18-34) show dramatically lower religiosity (~12-15% regular attendance) versus elderly (65+, ~40-45%). The trajectory is clearly toward further secularization.

**Doctrinal dissent:** Majority support for civil unions, widespread contraception use, and growing support for euthanasia legalization demonstrate that even self-identified Catholics reject Church teaching on major issues.
