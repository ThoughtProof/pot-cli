# Synthesized Answer: Differences Between Blondes and Brunettes

## The Core Answer

The difference between blondes and brunettes is fundamentally **melanin concentration in hair follicles** ‚Äî and almost nothing else that survives rigorous scrutiny.

**Biochemistry:** Brunette hair contains roughly 5‚Äì10√ó more eumelanin (dark pigment) than blonde hair. Blonde hair has low eumelanin with relatively more pheomelanin (yellow-red pigment). This is the primary measurable, reproducible difference.

**Genetics:** The key genes driving blonde vs. brunette hair are **OCA2/HERC2** (chromosome 15), **KITLG**, **TYRP1**, and **IRF4** ‚Äî not primarily MC1R, which is more strongly associated with red hair. Blondism is polygenic and recessive, occurring in roughly 2% of the global population but concentrated in Northern European populations due to founder effects and possible sexual selection.

**Physiological correlates (modest, confounded):**
- **UV sensitivity:** Lighter hair correlates with lighter skin, which increases melanoma risk ‚Äî but skin pigmentation drives this, not hair color directly
- **Vitamin D synthesis:** Lighter-skinned individuals (who tend to be blonde) may synthesize vitamin D more efficiently in low-sunlight environments ‚Äî a plausible evolutionary advantage at northern latitudes
- **Hair texture:** Blonde hair tends to be finer in diameter on average, though precise figures are poorly sourced

**What is NOT different:** Intelligence, personality, behavior. No GWAS has ever linked hair color to cognitive or personality traits with statistical significance.
