# What Does AD Mean?

**AD stands for "Anno Domini,"** Latin for **"in the year of our Lord."** The full phrase is *Anno Domini Nostri Jesu Christi* ("in the year of our Lord Jesus Christ"). It designates years counted forward from the traditionally calculated birth year of Jesus Christ.

## Key Facts

- **Introduced:** 525 by the monk **Dionysius Exiguus**, to replace the **Diocletian era** in Easter table computations
- **Popularized:** By the Venerable Bede in his *Ecclesiastical History of the English People* (731), which spread the system across Europe
- **Standard by:** The 9th‚Äì10th centuries in Europe; globally entrenched after the Gregorian calendar reform (1582)
- **Paired with:** BC ("Before Christ") for years before the epoch
- **No year 0** exists in this system ‚Äî 1 BC is immediately followed by 1 AD
- **Modern alternative:** CE ("Common Era") is numerically identical and used in many academic/secular contexts

## Common Misconception: "After Death"

This is **wrong.** If AD meant "After Death," the ~30‚Äì33 years of Jesus's life would fall into neither BC nor AD ‚Äî creating an unnamed gap. AD counts from birth, not death.

## Historical Accuracy Note

Dionysius's calculation was likely off by 4‚Äì7 years. Most scholars place Jesus's birth between 6‚Äì4 BC, based on evidence including the death of Herod the Great (typically dated to 4 BC). The system persists regardless because it's a calendar convention, not a historical claim.
