# Are Books Still Banned by Law in England?

## Synthesized Answer

**Yes, but narrowly and indirectly.** England has no official index of banned books. Instead, it has legal mechanisms that can criminalize the publication, distribution, or (in limited cases) possession of specific material. The concept of a "banned book" in England is murkier than the question implies.

## The Active Legal Mechanisms

**1. Obscene Publications Act 1959/1964** ‚Äî Material deemed to "deprave and corrupt" can be seized and destroyed by court order. This law remains on the books but is **largely dormant for textual works** since the late 1970s. It is still occasionally applied to extreme image-based pornographic material, not mainstream literature.

**2. Terrorism Act 2000 (s.58) & 2006 (s.2)** ‚Äî This is the **primary contemporary mechanism**. Possessing information "likely to be useful to a person committing or preparing an act of terrorism" is an offence ‚Äî but intent and context matter critically. Texts like AQAP's *Inspire* magazine and certain extremist tracts have led to prosecutions, but **no book is automatically "contraband"** in the way a controlled substance would be. The Anarchist Cookbook, for example, is not formally banned ‚Äî prosecution requires demonstrating intent or preparatory conduct.

**3. Official Secrets Acts** ‚Äî Can functionally suppress books containing classified information, as in the *Spycatcher* case (1980s), though that particular injunction was eventually lifted.

**4. Injunctions and super-injunctions** ‚Äî Courts can issue orders preventing publication of specific material. These are arguably a more active contemporary mechanism than the OPA, though they target specific texts case-by-case rather than creating standing bans.

**5. Libel law ("libel chill")** ‚Äî England's historically plaintiff-friendly defamation laws have suppressed books through litigation risk, though the Defamation Act 2013 reforms have somewhat reduced this effect. Not a "ban" per se, but a real suppression mechanism.

## What Is NOT Banned

*Lolita*, *The Satanic Verses*, *Spycatcher*, *Lady Chatterley's Lover*, and *Last Exit to Brooklyn* are all **legally available** in England today. *Mein Kampf* is sold freely in annotated/scholarly editions.

## The Honest Bottom Line

- **No maintained list** of banned books exists in England
- A small number of extreme texts (terrorism-related, CSAM-adjacent) face active prosecution ‚Äî primarily for **distribution and intent**, not mere possession
- The Obscene Publications Act is theoretically active but functionally dormant for books
- **Corporate delisting** (Amazon, retailers removing titles) is arguably a more common and impactful form of book suppression today than state action ‚Äî and operates with zero legal oversight
