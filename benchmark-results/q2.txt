## Concrete Position

**Ostriches do NOT bury their heads in the sand ‚Äî that's a myth.** Their primary defense is **running** (up to ~70 km/h / 43 mph), and if cornered, they deliver **powerful forward kicks** with clawed feet capable of killing large animals, including humans. ## Actual Threat Response (Verified Hierarchy)

**4. Group Vigilance:** Ostriches live in groups and use their exceptional eyesight (eyes ~5 cm diameter, among the largest of any land vertebrate) for early threat detection.

## The Myth's Origin

Pliny the Elder (AD 77, *Naturalis Historia*) described ostriches as believing they were hidden when their head was concealed. The myth likely arose from observing head-lowering during feeding, egg-turning, or the flat-lying camouflage posture. From a distance, the body blends in and the lowered head disappears ‚Äî creating the illusion of burial.

## What Can Go Wrong With This Information

- **False safety around ostriches:** They are genuinely dangerous. Farm and zoo workers suffer serious injuries; tourists who approach too close risk lethal kicks.
- **Underestimating intelligence:** The myth frames ostriches as stupid. Their vision-based vigilance system is actually well-adapted for open savanna survival.
- **Enclosed spaces increase danger:** On farms or in fenced areas, ostriches cannot flee and become significantly more aggressive.
