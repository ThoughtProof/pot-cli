## Why Cold Snaps Still Happen on a Warming Planet

Arctic warming can destabilize the jet stream, causing polar vortex disruptions that send extreme cold air into mid-latitudes. **However â€” and this is important** â€” the strength of this Arctic-midlatitude linkage remains actively debated in climate science. Some research supports it (Cohen et al.), but it is **not settled consensus**. The proposals that presented this as established fact overstated the science; the most honest treatment acknowledged "some research suggests" this connection.

Key point: warming shifts the entire temperature distribution hotter. Cold extremes still occur, but **record highs now outpace record lows by roughly 2:1** in the U.S. (Meehl et al. 2009 and updates). The dice are loaded toward heat, even if cold rolls still happen.
